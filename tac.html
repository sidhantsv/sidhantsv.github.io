NASA FSGC Hybrid Motor High-Powered Rocket

Class of the field, and winner of the NASA FSGC rocket competitions

Hybrid motor powered rockets that were designed for two different NASA FSGC competitions in 2019. The rockets were designed with in-house solid PVC fuel grain for a higher maximum altitude, and nitrous oxide as oxidizer. Know more about it below!

As part of the Mechanic team, I played a major role in various aspects of these rockets, from component design, static firing testing, to data recording, communication and verification.

About Florida Space Grant Consortium

The Florida Space Grant Consortium (FSGC) was formed in 1989, when NASA implemented the National Space Grant College and Fellowship Program. FSGC was one of the sixteen founding Space Grant Consortia. Currently there are now Space Grant programs active in all fifty states as well as Puerto Rico and Washington D.C. When combined with the Land Grant and Sea Grant Programs, Space Grant forms the final leg of a triad of federally mandated programs addressing critical national needs in education, research, and service.

  About FSGC Hybrid Motor Rocket Competitions

The objective of the competition is to build and launch a hybrid powered rocket with a hybrid motor rated “G” or from a lower class (160 Newton seconds or less). The competition has two categories. The first category consists of launching a hybrid rocket to the maximum altitude. The second category challenges the teams to fly their rocket closest to 2,000 feet in altitude.

  About the rockets



  • Read the club article on the win

The twin rockets were researched and built in-house at Florida Atlantic University by the Technology and Aerospace Club. Prototype rockets of the type were tested extensively beforehand at amateur events and one-off testing days, and finally flew in Palm Bay, FL for the FSGC Competitions in March 2019.

Within the Mechanical Team, I had the privilege of contributing to multiple aspects of the rockets. My role encompassed a diverse range of tasks, including the CAD modeling of the rockets in Solidworks, runningthermal analysis to meet the specifications of both competition categories, fabrication of components of the rocket, Arduino testing and coding, flying tests, prototyping, and meticulous data recording.
